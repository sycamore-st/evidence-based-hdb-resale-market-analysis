# Section 3: Policy Analysis

## A. Is Yishun the cheapest?
Yishun ranks **#3** on post-2018 overall median resale price and **#2** among 4-room flats.
That means the evidence should be framed as relative affordability, not a blanket claim that Yishun is the cheapest nationwide.

## B. Have flat sizes shrunk?
Controlled year trend coefficient: **-0.077 sqm per year** (p-value 0.0000).
Negative coefficient suggests flats sold have become smaller after controlling for town and flat type.

## C. Did Downtown Line Stage 2 raise prices?
DiD interaction effect: **2.4%** with p-value **0.0000**.
Treated towns used in the base case: BUKIT BATOK, BUKIT PANJANG, BUKIT TIMAH, CENTRAL AREA, KALLANG/WHAMPOA, QUEENSTOWN, TOA PAYOH.

## D. Are Sengkang and Punggol linked to COE prices?
Estimated log-log elasticity of resale prices to COE premiums: **0.136** (p-value 0.0000).
This should be presented as differential sensitivity with controls, not proof of household-level substitution behavior.
The sign is not guaranteed by the prompt alone: a positive interaction supports a housing-car substitution story, while a negative interaction would support a pure car-affordability squeeze.
