# Do Outer HDB Towns React More To COE Shocks Than Central Towns?

## Business Context

A common claim is that buyers trade off housing location and car affordability. If so, COE swings may affect outer-town resale demand differently from central towns.

## Constraint

The right test is differential sensitivity, not raw co-movement. Both groups can rise together under macro conditions.

## Results

The interaction term is the key decision metric. A positive estimate indicates stronger outer-town response when COE rises; a negative estimate indicates weaker response.

Across models, the directional evidence supports meaningful differential sensitivity rather than identical response patterns.

## Interpretation

The housing-plus-car substitution narrative is plausible in the data, but should be communicated as relative sensitivity evidence, not a universal household-level behavioral rule.

## Recommended Decision

Track COE exposure explicitly in outer-town pricing strategy and scenario planning, and monitor whether this sensitivity changes across market cycles.
