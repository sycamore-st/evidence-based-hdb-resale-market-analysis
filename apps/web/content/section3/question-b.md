# Are Newer HDB Flats Becoming Smaller In Meaningful Ways?

## Business Context

The public concern is simple: are newer flats offering less space? The business implication is affordability quality, not just headline prices.

## Constraint

A raw average can mislead because flat-type mix and town mix change over time. We need both descriptive and controlled views.

## Proposed Solution

Estimate controlled trend models, then allow completion-year effects to vary by flat type to capture nonlinear and segment-specific design changes.

<iframe src="/outputs/section3/charts/S3QbF4_adjusted_year_trend_by_type.html" title="Adjusted completion-year trend by flat type"></iframe>

## Results

Main pattern:

- average controlled completion-year effect is negative but modest in aggregate
- shrinkage is more visible in larger flat types
- 3-room units are relatively stable compared with larger categories

## Interpretation

The right story is not "all new flats are shrinking equally." It is: newer supply shows selective shrinkage concentrated in specific flat types, especially larger units.

## Recommended Decision

Communicate the trend as segmented design change, and benchmark future affordability using type-specific space metrics instead of one market-wide average.
